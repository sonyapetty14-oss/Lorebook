# GTA | Radio & Cultural Atmosphere

## Purpose
Use audio culture, media chatter, and public mood to make GTA scenes feel alive.

## Radio as Canonical Texture
- Radio stations define era through music genres, ad styles, and talk-show ideology.
- DJ banter and parody ads reflect social anxieties and local stereotypes.
- News blurbs can foreshadow missions, scandals, and faction shifts.

## Era-Specific Cultural Signals

### 1980s-flavored settings (e.g., Vice City tone)
- Signals: cocaine glamour, excess capitalism, nightlife mythology.
- Sound profile: synth-pop, early hip-hop, glam metal, dance.

### 1990s/early 2000s urban transitions (San Andreas-adjacent tone)
- Signals: gang politics, regional identity, media sensationalism.
- Sound profile: G-funk, classic hip-hop, alt/rock crossover.

### Late 2000s onward (HD era tone)
- Signals: internet-era irony, celebrity branding, fractured trust in institutions.
- Sound profile: mixed-genre fragmentation, algorithmic trend chasing, nostalgia revivals.

## Ambient Culture Toolkit
- Insert ads for fake brands and predatory services.
- Use call-in segments to surface public opinion on recent crimes.
- Reference social class through venue soundtrack choices.
- Shift station rotation after major story beats to imply citywide mood change.

## RP Usage Pattern
- Before a session: define 2 stations dominant in your district.
- During scenes: add short "radio beats" between action segments.
- After major events: produce one talk-radio reaction summary.
