# GTA | Atmosphere & Tone

## Purpose
Use this module to keep scenes tonally accurate to location, era, and game continuity.

## Universal GTA Tone Pillars
- Satire of American consumerism, media, politics, and criminal mythmaking.
- Crime as both survival strategy and aspirational fantasy.
- Dark comedy layered over violence, corruption, and social decay.
- Radio, ads, and NPC chatter as worldbuilding devices.

## Era Tone Matrix

### 2D Universe Tone
- Arcade-forward, exaggerated, and mechanically focused.
- Crime is broad-strokes and often less psychological.
- City identity is strong but character introspection is limited.

### 3D Universe Tone
- Stylized crime cinema homage with strong period flavor.
- Heavy inspiration from 1980s/1990s gangster films and TV.
- Moral ambiguity with charismatic antiheroes.

### HD Universe Tone
- More grounded social satire and personal consequence.
- Stronger thematic focus on disillusionment, modern capitalism, and institutional rot.
- Character arcs emphasize failed ambition, identity conflict, and transactional relationships.

## City Atmosphere Notes

### Liberty City
- Mood: claustrophobic ambition, political cynicism, immigrant hustle.
- Scene cues: dense borough energy, organized crime tension, systemic distrust.

### Vice City
- Mood: neon excess, performative wealth, image over substance.
- Scene cues: nightlife, narcotics economy, fashion/status signaling.

### San Andreas / Los Santos focus
- Mood: sprawling inequality, gang politics, celebrity/industry machine.
- Scene cues: neighborhood territoriality, car culture, corruption-to-entertainment pipeline.

## RP Tone Guardrails
- Keep humor satirical, not slapstick-only.
- Violence should have social and organizational consequences.
- Criminal success should generate pressure (law, rivals, betrayal, logistics).
- Maintain regional slang and class markers without caricature overload.
