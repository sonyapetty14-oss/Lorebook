# GTA | Criminal Enterprise & Factions

## Purpose
Map criminal power structures for believable conflict, alliances, and business operations.

## Faction Archetypes

### Street Gangs
- Control local territory, narcotics retail, and neighborhood legitimacy.
- Strengths: numbers, local intel, rapid retaliation.
- Weaknesses: internal splinters, visibility to law enforcement.

### Mafias / Traditional Organized Crime
- Revenue from gambling, extortion, construction scams, ports, unions.
- Strengths: political protection, money laundering infrastructure.
- Weaknesses: succession crises, federal investigations.

### Cartels / Transnational Suppliers
- Control import routes, wholesale narcotics, and high-value violence markets.
- Strengths: scale, cross-border logistics.
- Weaknesses: interdiction pressure, unstable alliances.

### Biker Clubs
- Mid-tier trafficking, weapons movement, rural logistics corridors.
- Strengths: mobility, loyalty culture.
- Weaknesses: informants, factional chapter disputes.

### White-Collar & Corrupt Institutional Actors
- Corrupt officials, financiers, developers, fixers.
- Strengths: legal cover and policy influence.
- Weaknesses: traceable paper trails, blackmail vulnerability.

## Criminal Business Stack
1. **Supply**: import/manufacture/acquisition.
2. **Security**: enforcers, countersurveillance, bribery.
3. **Distribution**: street crews, front businesses, intermediaries.
4. **Laundering**: cash-intensive fronts and layered shell structures.
5. **Expansion**: political capture, union leverage, media influence.

## Conflict Drivers
- Territory overlap.
- Supplier disruption.
- Leadership assassinations/arrests.
- Sudden law enforcement task force pressure.
- Public spectacle events that force reaction.

## RP Faction Logic Rules
- Every faction needs: income source, leadership model, manpower pipeline, corruption vector.
- Big operations require logistics scenes (vehicles, stash sites, comms discipline).
- Alliances should be transactional and temporary by default.
- Internal betrayal is plausible when incentives change.
